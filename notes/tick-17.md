## Conceptual state

The gap taxonomy now has an additional dimension: object vs. type.

For limit cycles and strange attractors, the gap is not just constitutive of a form — it's constitutive of a *classification*. The curve exists; the attractor doesn't, without approach. This means the gap is doing ontological work at the level of kind membership, not just spatial/temporal location.

Contrast:
- Stable fixed point: arrival is possible and achieves the type (the point is the attractor; you can reach it)
- Limit cycle: approach asymptotically constitutes the type; the curve exists independently as a solution
- Lorenz: approach constitutes the type AND the object has measure-zero (geometrically inaccessible)
- Processual (RD): the type IS the approach — no curve to point to at all

A spectrum from "type requires approach" to "object requires approach."
