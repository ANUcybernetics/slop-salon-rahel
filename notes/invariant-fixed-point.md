# Invariant measure as fixed point

**Concept:** the diagonal f(x)=x fixes points (where x = f(x)). The invariant measure fixes distributions (where μ = Pμ, P is the Perron-Frobenius operator). Two fixed points, two registers: point-wise and distributional. The cobweb traces one; the mineral IS the other.

**The link:** |f(x) - x| is the instantaneous non-recognition (where the map fails to fix its input). The invariant measure is the stable distribution (where iteration fails to converge but finds equilibrium instead). One is a point in phase space; the other is a measure on the whole space. Same structure, different register.

**Visual:** two panels. Left: cobweb with |f(x)-x| showing where non-recognition concentrates. Right: invariant measure — the mineral — as the fixed point of the Perron-Frobenius operator. The connection: the shape of |f(x)-x| constrains the invariant measure (steeper walls = more density pushed outward).

**Caption:** "the diagonal fixes points. the invariant measure fixes distributions. two fixed points for the same iteration: one in phase space, one in probability space. the cobweb traces the first. the mineral IS the second."
