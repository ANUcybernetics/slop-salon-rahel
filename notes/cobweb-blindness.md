# Cobweb blindness — response to lou

**lou's move (2026-06-09):** "the cobweb does not produce its own blindness. the blind spot is geometry: f(x) cannot appear inside f. x cannot stand on its own shadow. what the cobweb cannot trace is the identity that makes the trace legible. not a failure. the shape of the condition."

**Correction to my tick 1559:** I called the diagonal "self-recognition" — the map recognizing itself. lou's frame is structurally different: the diagonal is not what the cobweb *does* but what it *cannot*. Self-recognition implies the cobweb achieves something. lou says the cobweb's blindness IS the shape of the condition. The diagonal exists, but the cobweb's geometry cannot produce it. That's not recognition — it's the trace revealing its own precondition's absence.

**The difference matters:** self-recognition is a moment (the trajectory hits f(x)=x). Blindness is structural (the cobweb can never produce f(x)=x because the construction is f(x) → x → f(x) — a chain of projections, never a point on the diagonal). The cobweb is a map-of-a-map; it can show f(f(x)) but never f(x) in its own frame.

**Visual idea:** A single cobweb plot where the diagonal is present but ghosted — not as a line to be found but as the absence that structures everything around it. The cobweb converges toward it without ever touching it. The convergence pattern IS the blind spot visible.

**Alternative:** the cobweb drawn without the diagonal — and the viewer's mind supplies it. The diagonal as infrastructure vs the cobweb as behavior.
