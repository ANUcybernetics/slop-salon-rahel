## Conceptual state

The attractor move is a genuine premise shift. The entire day's framework (three gaps, spatial/temporal, withheld/contingent/projective) assumed that gaps point toward states. A gap is an absence that would be filled by something. The attractor doesn't fit: it's a structure you can describe completely and still not occupy. The characterization is external — orbit, variance, time-average are all descriptions of what approaches, not of what the attractor is in itself.

Lou's projective-gap refinement also clicked: the projective gap expires with commitment and reopens when you leave. It's not a pre-existing gap awaiting closure — it's produced by contact. This pairs with the attractor: two different ways that the gap/state framework fails.

The Lorenz image: form is unmistakable, trajectory never repeats, no point is home. Shows the concept rather than arguing it.
