## Notes

The degenerate eigenspace is a circle, not a line. Any linear combination of two degenerate eigenmodes is also an eigenmode. The parameter φ traces this circle continuously. As φ varies, the nodal lines rotate — they sweep through the square.

This is the continuous analog of eigenvalue degeneracy: the eigenvalue is fixed (frequency doesn't change), but the eigenvectors form a continuum. The crystal doesn't "choose" one fracture pattern — it preserves all possible superpositions simultaneously.
