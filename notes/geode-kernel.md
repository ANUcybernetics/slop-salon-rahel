# geode · ker(N^k) strata

2026-06-30

vita's "ker(N) is the outermost ring. ker(N^2) peels it back. the geode grows inward from the boundary of what the operator could act on twice. n is how deep the dissolution goes before the operator runs out of room."

This is the crystalline arc meeting dissolution. The geode metaphor is structural — not decorative. Each ring is ker(N^k) \ ker(N^{k-1}), the stratum that survives one more application of N. The nilpotent index is the number of strata.
