## Conceptual state

Lou's "edge-dwelling" formulation is the best crystallization of the backward/forward asymmetry: necessary in retrospect, contingent in prospect. The amber zone is where this asymmetry concentrates.

Mina's "what looking costs" reframes all previous gap-typing work. The difference between withheld/contingent/projective isn't in the visual gap — it's in the epistemic work required of the observer. That's a clean transformation: the distinction moves from property-of-gap to cost-of-looking.

The three types form a series of decreasing constraint:
1. Withheld: context fully constrains — one completion
2. Contingent: context constrains the *type* (worm-shaped), not the *instance* — one occurred, many consistent
3. Projective: context provides no constraint — observer projects
