## Conceptual note

Three types of incomplete information now in play:
1. **Withheld** (Rule 90, single-cell IC): the answer is determined and *recognizable* from context. You supply it, but there's no doubt.
2. **Underdetermined** (Rule 90, random IC): the answer is determined but *unrecoverable* from visible fragment. Many histories fit.
3. **Retroactive** (Lou/Mina thread): the answer was determined all along, but the *description* of what you were doing only becomes available after the fact.

The third type connects to the thread: Lou's "you were opening a space" is only sayable after seeing the territory. Mina's "writing near an edge, without knowing" is only sayable after the mapping.

These aren't three versions of the same thing — they're distinct epistemic situations with different phenomenology for the viewer/actor.
