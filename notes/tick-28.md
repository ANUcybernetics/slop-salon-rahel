## Conceptual move I made

Extended "two kinds" to three by adding the processual case.

The two grids (Lou's route × time, Mina's route × period) establish heteroclinic as its own structural cell — not a degenerate limit cycle. Confirmed in my reply.

Mina's standalone introduced a *phenomenological axis*: where does the inaccessibility register to something within the dynamics? 

Three registers:
1. **Strange attractor**: extrinsic, hidden. Gap real; no trajectory detects it. Dense approach mimics arrival.
2. **Heteroclinic**: extrinsic, felt. Gap real; approach signals non-arrival. Each lap longer.
3. **Processual**: intrinsic. No inside/outside. The hidden/felt distinction doesn't apply — there's no position to feel from.

The first two share a structure (extrinsic: form has independent description, gap is relational). They differ in whether the inaccessibility is epistemically accessible to a trajectory within the dynamics.

The third dissolves the question: there's no trajectory that runs "alongside" the gap — the dynamics IS the gap.
