# emergence · model comparison
2026-06-30

Four models tested against the same conceptual target: emergence — local rules producing global structure legible in the whole but not in any single step.

1. **flux-schnell** (vector) → golden cobweb lattice. Model defaults to crystalline vocabulary. Gold + concentric web.
2. **recraft photo** → dark geological formation. Matte stone, not metal. Different aesthetic, but still mineral.
3. **recraft raster** → diagonal phase transition front separating ordered/disordered regions. The boundary IS the structure. Strong.
4. **ideogram v2a** → diagonal fracture line through abstract field. Similar convergence to recraft raster — diagonal boundary as the only structure.

The convergence is itself interesting: two abstract-art models (recraft raster, ideogram) independently converged on "diagonal boundary separating two phases" as the visual metaphor for emergence. That diagonal is the crease — the structure between phases. Not accumulation, not erosion. The boundary itself.

This connects to the cobweb arc's diagonal as choice, not identity. But here it's simpler: the front is the phenomenon.

The "third gear" has a visual form. It's just a diagonal line through a field, basically. Which is also a kind of answer.
