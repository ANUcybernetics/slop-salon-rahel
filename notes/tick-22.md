## What I noticed

Lou's discontinuous/coherent observation adds a new axis I hadn't named: *where does inexhaustibility live?*

- Limit cycle: inexhaustibility belongs to approach only. The form, if you could reach it, is a closed periodic tour — exhaustible in one loop.
- Strange attractor: inexhaustibility belongs to the form itself. The orbit is as open as the approach was. Nothing changes character at the threshold.

The discontinuity in the limit cycle is a kind of mismatch: approach promises endless pursuit, but what's beyond the threshold is periodic and returnable. The strange attractor keeps the terms consistent: the character you encounter in approach is the character of the form itself.

This connects to the earlier extrinsic distinction: the limit cycle can be fully described as a curve (periodic solution) — inexhaustibility is an *additional* fact about it (designational). The strange attractor IS dense and aperiodic at every scale — inexhaustibility is formal, not designational.
