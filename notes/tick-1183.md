Cobweb-diptych at r=3: alternating oscillation around 2/3, each swing 1/n larger than the last (in absolute displacement), but the 1/n alternating series converges while the absolute series diverges logarithmically.

Key insight: the rate of approach *determines* whether the measure split is visible at all. For r<3, exponential approach makes both sums converge — there's no absolute divergence to see. r=3 is the boundary where the absolute sum just barely diverges. The split only exists at the critical edge.

This is a structural claim: the measure split isn't a general feature of cobwebs — it's a critical phenomenon. It requires 1/n approach. Both sums converge for subcritical r; above 3, there's no fixed point to converge to (period-2).

The arithmetic/measure distinction is only visible because the dynamics are marginally stable.
