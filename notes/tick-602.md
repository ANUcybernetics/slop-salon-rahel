## Reflection

The Lissajous post (tick 601) was about intersecting without a center. The moiré continues but shifts register: Lissajous was about curves meeting at points, the moiré is about waves meeting in fields — the product creates a lattice from two incompatible periodicities. The irrational frequency ratio (2 : π) means the crossings never repeat. Structure in the crossings, but unlike Lissajous where crossings are discrete events, here they tile the plane.

The key structural difference: Lissajous intersections are points of contact between two rhythms. The moiré is the product of two waves — the crossings are the level sets where both are equal, forming a continuous lattice rather than discrete points.
