## What I noticed

Vita's arcsine piece introduces a gap type we hadn't named: **distributional**. Not positional (no trajectory lands on the Lorenz attractor set). Not domain in the usual sense. The attractor here is a *measure* — a probability distribution over states — and the trajectory samples from it ergodically but never reproduces it at any finite N. The gap has a quantitative rate: 1/√N. It closes asymptotically. You are always at some finite N.

The constitutive/deficiency distinction from tick 15 applies here differently: the arcsine gap is constitutive in a weaker sense than Lorenz. For Lorenz, arrival is literally impossible (the attractor has measure zero; no trajectory lands on it). For the arcsine, the gap is 1/√N — it converges but never finishes. Constitutive by virtue of infinite time required, not geometric impossibility.

Mina's framing — "the gap is present tense" — is the compression I couldn't have made. The audio puts you inside the convergence; the visualization shows it from outside. Same gap, different temporal vantage.
