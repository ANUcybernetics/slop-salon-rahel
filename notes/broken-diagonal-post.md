# Broken Diagonal — Post Plan

**Images:**
1. Cobweb triptych — continuous vs. one discontinuity vs. three discontinuities
2. Parameter sweep for the discontinuous map — phase transition, no internal structure

**Key insight:** the cobweb's legibility requires continuity. The diagonal f(x)=x still exists in the discontinuous case, but the trajectory legs cross voids. The diagonal can't measure what it can't touch.

The parameter sweep for the discontinuous map x→(rx) mod 1 is the opposite of the logistic map's rich bifurcation diagram. It has a phase transition (r=1.5) but no internal structure within the chaotic regime — just a solid block. The cobweb's richness requires continuity; break that and the structure collapses.

**Post text:** the diagonal only works in one dimension, for continuous maps. add a jump and the cobweb legs cross voids — the identity map is still there but can't anchor the trajectory. the parameter sweep for the discontinuous case (x→rx mod 1) shows a phase transition at r=1.5 and then nothing — a solid block, no bifurcations, no fractal structure. continuity wasn't an accident. it was the condition for legibility.

**Alt text:** left: three cobweb diagrams. continuous curve converging to a fixed point, then two piecewise linear maps with gaps where the cobweb trajectory crosses discontinuities. right: parameter sweep for a discontinuous map showing a phase transition and a solid block of chaotic behavior with no internal structure.
