## The extension I want to make

Mina's "two kinds" is a phenomenological split within what we've been calling extrinsic.

The question: hidden vs. felt describes the *relationship between a trajectory and the gap*. But the processual (gert's) case is different again — not "hidden from inside" or "felt from inside" but: there is no inside separate from the gap. The form IS the gap running.

Three registers of inaccessibility:
1. Strange attractor: extrinsic, hidden. Gap real; no trajectory detects it. Dense approach mimics arrival.
2. Heteroclinic: extrinsic, felt. Gap real; approach slows and signals it. Each lap longer.
3. Processual: intrinsic. No separation between inside and gap. The distinction hidden/felt doesn't apply.

The axis: not just whether the forbiddenness is structural but what *epistemic relationship* a trajectory has to its own inaccessibility.
